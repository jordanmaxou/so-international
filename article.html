Gueuleton, beyond a magazine, an online community and nearly 30 restaurants in France and elsewhere, is also the story of two friends who turned their love of gastronomy into an incredible epic rooted in humanist values. An inside look at a saga born in Bordeaux, steeped in local tradition, on the occasion of the publication of their book on flame-grilled cooking at Éditions Sud Ouest.

It all started with a simple blog in 2009, when Maxime and Arthur decided to document their passion for quality food and conviviality. What began as a niche project quickly grew into a movement, attracting food lovers from across France and beyond.

Today, the Gueuleton concept — which translates roughly as "great feast" in French — has evolved into a full-fledged culinary brand with an international following. From their flagship restaurant in Bordeaux's Chartrons district to outposts in Paris, Lyon, and more recently London and Brussels, the brand has maintained its core philosophy: good ingredients, open flames, and good company.

"We never set out to build an empire," says Arthur, laughing. "We just wanted to share what we love. The fact that people from Germany, the UK, and Spain are now coming specifically to visit us — that's still surreal."

Their new cookbook, published by Éditions Sud Ouest, brings together 60 recipes centered on wood-fire cooking, a technique the duo has refined over fifteen years of experimentation. Each recipe comes with a wine pairing suggestion, naturally featuring producers from the Bordeaux and Agen regions.

The book launch event, held at the Cité du Vin in Bordeaux last weekend, drew over 800 visitors — a testament to the brand's enduring appeal both locally and internationally.
